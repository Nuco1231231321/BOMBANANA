## The 10 Most Common Beginner Mistakes

Every BOMBANANA! team makes these mistakes. Here's what they are, why they happen, and exactly how to fix each one.

![Common Beginner Mistakes](/images/screenshots/ss_0345ddc6d194e6528b764cf488ca5ead157b7995.thumb.jpg)

### 1. Acting Without Confirmation

**What happens:** The Blind Monkey hears an instruction and immediately cuts/presses without repeating it back.

**Why it's bad:** If the Blind misheard or miscounted, the bomb takes a strike with zero chance to correct.

**The fix:** Always use the 3-step loop:
- Deaf: *"Cut the third wire from the left"*
- Blind: *"Third wire from left — confirm?"*
- Deaf: *"Confirmed — cut"*

This takes 1 second and prevents 90% of mistakes.

### 2. Looking at the Bomb Instead of the Mute

**What happens:** The Deaf Monkey stares at the bomb watching the Blind's hands, missing the Mute's next gesture.

**Why it's bad:** The Mute's gesture is the ONLY source of information. Missing it means the Deaf has nothing to tell the Blind.

**The fix:** The Deaf's default gaze should be on the Mute. Glance at the bomb only when:
- Verifying the Blind is touching the correct position
- Checking module completion (green light)
- Reading the timer

### 3. Gesturing Too Small

**What happens:** The Mute makes small hand gestures near their chest that the Deaf cannot see clearly.

**Why it's bad:** The Deaf misinterprets, gives wrong instructions, bomb explodes.

**The fix:** Exaggerate everything. Full arm extension. Gesture at chest-to-head height. If the Deaf squints, go bigger.

### 4. Solving Modules in the Wrong Order

**What happens:** The team tackles the easiest modules first, leaving the hardest module for the final 30 seconds with 2 strikes already used.

**Why it's bad:** You want maximum time and zero strikes for the hardest puzzle. Doing it last under pressure is the worst scenario.

**The fix:** After the Blind's sweep, the Deaf identifies all modules. The team agrees: **hardest module first.**

### 5. Using External Voice Chat

**What happens:** The team uses Discord for voice chat instead of the in-game system with role restrictions.

**Why it's bad:** The Mute can now speak. The Deaf can now hear. Both restrictions are gone. The game becomes a simple bomb puzzle — you've removed the core mechanic that makes BOMBANANA! unique and challenging.

**The fix:** Use in-game voice chat. The restrictions ARE the game.

### 6. Not Establishing Gestures Before Playing

**What happens:** The team jumps into a bomb with no agreed gesture vocabulary. The Mute frantically waves hands; the Deaf guesses randomly.

**Why it's bad:** Without shared gesture meanings, the Mute's communication is useless.

**The fix:** Spend 5 minutes before the first bomb agreeing on:
- Color indicators (number mapping or color cards)
- Number gestures (fingers for 1-10)
- Action gestures (cut, press, hold, release, rotate)
- Yes/no signals

### 7. Ignoring the Timer Until It's Too Late

**What happens:** The team gets absorbed in a difficult module and doesn't notice there are 5 seconds left.

**Why it's bad:** Bomb explodes with 2 modules remaining.

**The fix:** The Deaf announces the timer at:
- **60 seconds:** "One minute — 2 modules to go"
- **30 seconds:** "30 seconds"
- **10 seconds:** "EXECUTE NOW — no confirmation"

### 8. Not Debriefing After Failure

**What happens:** The bomb explodes. Everyone sighs. "Let's try again." Same mistakes happen.

**Why it's bad:** You're not learning from your failures.

**The fix:** After every bomb, spend 2 minutes:
- What went well?
- What went wrong?
- What should we change?

### 9. Wrong Module Manual Section

**What happens:** The Mute looks up the wrong module type in the manual and gestures the wrong solution.

**Why it's bad:** The team follows instructions perfectly — for a different module. Strike.

**The fix:** The Deaf clearly identifies each module by name before the Mute opens the manual. The Mute confirms by pointing at the module → matching section in the manual → thumbs up.

### 10. Panic Spiraling

**What happens:** One mistake causes tension. Tension causes another mistake. The cycle accelerates until the bomb explodes.

**Why it's bad:** Panic destroys communication more effectively than any bomb module.

**The fix:** 
- The Deaf is the team's voice of reason. If you sound calm, the Blind stays calm.
- After a strike, take a 2-second breath. Reset. Continue.
- Remember: It's a game about monkeys defusing a banana bomb. Laugh.
