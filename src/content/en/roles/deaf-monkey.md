## The Deaf Monkey 🙉

**Role:** *"Hear No Evil"*

The Deaf Monkey is the **critical communication bridge** in BOMBANANA! You are the only player who can both see the bomb clearly AND speak instructions. But you hear absolutely nothing — no voice chat, no game sounds, not even the bomb's ticking timer.

![BOMBANANA! Team Communication](/images/screenshots/ss_353f30e2c12b406ab6f48642e6c21b2c5ffe1d72.thumb.jpg)

### What You Can Do

- **See everything** — Every module, every wire color, every screen readout, every timer digit
- **Speak** — You give verbal instructions to the Blind Monkey
- **Observe the Mute** — You are the only one who can interpret the Mute's gestures

### What You Cannot Do

- **Hear anything** — No voice from teammates, no game audio cues, no bomb ticking sounds
- **Touch the bomb** — Only the Blind Monkey can physically interact

## Your Position in the Chain

```
MUTE MONKEY (gestures) → YOU (interpret + speak) → BLIND MONKEY (touch the bomb)
```

You are the **translator**. Information comes in through your eyes (two sources: the bomb and the Mute's gestures) and goes out through your mouth. Every word you say determines whether the bomb gets defused or explodes.

## Core Strategy

### 1. Face the Mute, Not the Bomb

Your default gaze should be on the Mute Monkey. Glance at the bomb only when you need specific visual information. The Mute is your primary data source.

### 2. Speak in Short, Precise Commands

The Blind Monkey needs absolute clarity. Use this format:
- **[Action] + [Position] + [Object]**
- *"Cut — third wire from left — the blue one"*
- *"Press and hold — bottom right button — for 3 seconds"*

### 3. Always Get Confirmation

Never let the Blind proceed without verbal confirmation:
- You: *"Cut the second wire from the right"*
- Blind: *"Second wire from right — cutting now?"*
- You: *"Confirmed — cut"*

### 4. Monitor the Bomb State

You are the only person who can see:
- **The countdown timer** — Announce remaining time regularly
- **Module status lights** — Which modules are completed (green) vs. pending (red)
- **Panic effects** — Visual glitches, screen shake, module transformations

## Reading the Mute's Gestures

This is the hardest part of the Deaf role. The Mute knows the solution but cannot speak. You must decode their body language.

### Gesture Vocabulary

| Information | Recommended Gesture |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Wire color: Red | Point to something red (clothing, a card, a sticker) |
| Wire color: Blue | Point to blue object or use pre-agreed blue hand shape |
| Number 1-5 | Hold up that many fingers |
| Number 6-10 | Two hands: 5 + remaining fingers |
| Left side | Point left with whole arm |
| Right side | Point right with whole arm |
| Cut action | Scissors motion with two fingers |
| Press action | Index finger pushing down |
| Hold action | Closed fist |
| Rotate action | Circular finger motion |
| Yes / Correct | Thumbs up |
| No / Wrong | Thumbs down or crossed arms |
| Repeat gesture | Circular "again" motion with hand |
| Urgent / Fast | Rapid waving |

### When You Don't Understand

If you cannot decode a gesture:
1. Shake your head side to side
2. The Mute will repeat or try a different gesture
3. Do not guess — a wrong interpretation leads to a wrong instruction

## The Communication Rhythm

Establish this loop with your Blind teammate:

1. **Observe** — Watch the Mute's gesture
2. **Decode** — Convert gesture to an instruction in your mind
3. **Speak** — Give the instruction to the Blind in clear, short words
4. **Listen for repeat** — The Blind should repeat it back
5. **Confirm or correct** — "Confirmed" or correct the misunderstanding
6. **Watch execution** — See the result on the bomb
7. **Report status** — Tell the Mute the result (thumbs up or down)

## Timer Management

The bomb's timer is your responsibility. Call out remaining time at these thresholds:
- **60 seconds**: "One minute remaining, 2 modules to go"
- **30 seconds**: "30 seconds — focus on the last module"
- **10 seconds**: "10 seconds — execute now, no confirmations needed"

## Module-Specific Guidance

### Wire Modules
- Identify the target wire by color and position
- Guide the Blind's hand: "Move your hand up... a little left... stop there — that wire"
- Only say "cut" after position is confirmed

### Button Modules
- Describe the button by grid position: "Second row, third column"
- Help the Blind navigate by touch: "Move right two buttons from your current position"

### Sequence Modules
- Call out each step only when the previous step is complete
- "Step 1 of 4: Press top button. ... Done. Step 2 of 4: Flip left switch."

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why | Fix |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Looking at the bomb instead of the Mute | Natural tendency to watch the action | Face the Mute, glance at bomb only when necessary |
| Over-explaining to the Blind | Trying to be helpful | Short, precise commands only |
| Guessing the Mute's meaning | Impatience | Ask for repeat — never guess |
| Missing timer announcements | Focused on communication | Set mental checkpoints every 15 seconds |
| Not reporting panic effects | Didn't notice | Scan all modules visually every 5 seconds |

## Advanced Techniques

### Dual Vision Training
Practice splitting your attention: peripheral vision on the bomb status while focused on the Mute's body language.

### Predictive Instruction
After several bombs with the same team, you will anticipate common patterns. Prepare instructions mentally before the Mute finishes gesturing.

### Stress Management
You are the team's voice of reason. If you sound calm, the Blind stays calm. If you sound panicked, the bomb will explode.
