## The Mute Monkey 🙊

**Role:** *"Speak No Evil"*

The Mute Monkey holds the **defusal manual** — you know exactly how to solve every module. But you cannot speak a single word. All communication must be through **gestures, body language, and pre-agreed signals**.

### What You Can Do

- **Read the manual** — you have the complete defusal instructions
- **See everything** — the bomb, your teammates, all visual information
- **Hear** your teammates
- **Gesture** — use any non-verbal communication

### What You Cannot Do

- **Speak** — no words, no sounds, no voice chat

## Core Strategy

### 1. Build Your Gesture Vocabulary

Before the game starts, establish clear gestures with the Deaf Monkey:

- **Colors**: Point to colored objects or hold up color-coded cards
- **Numbers**: Finger counting (agree on which system)
- **Directions**: Clear pointing (up, down, left, right)
- **Actions**: Cut, press, hold, flip, rotate — each needs a distinct gesture
- **Yes/No**: Thumbs up and thumbs down
- **Urgency**: Speed of gesture indicates time pressure

### 2. Pace Your Communication

- Start slow and deliberate — speed comes with practice
- Wait for the Deaf Monkey to acknowledge before moving to the next gesture
- If the Deaf looks confused, **repeat the gesture** rather than inventing a new one

### 3. Know Your Manual

The better you know the manual, the faster you can find the right solution and gesture it. Study the manual between games:

- Memorize common module solutions
- Learn the manual's organization for quick lookup
- Prepare gestures for each module type in advance

## Gesture Design Principles

1. **Distinctness** — Each gesture should be clearly different from others
2. **Simplicity** — Complex gestures cause errors under pressure
3. **Consistency** — Use the same gesture every time for the same thing
4. **Feedback** — Build in a way for the Deaf to signal "repeat" or "got it"

## Pre-Game Setup Checklist

- [ ] Agree on the gesture vocabulary with the Deaf Monkey
- [ ] Practice 3-5 key gestures before the first bomb
- [ ] Set up your manual in easy reach
- [ ] Position yourself where the Deaf Monkey can see you clearly
- [ ] Confirm the feedback signals (yes/no/repeat)

## Advanced Techniques

- **Gesture chaining** — combine multiple gestures into fluid sequences
- **Spatial referencing** — use your body position to represent bomb layout
- **Temporal signaling** — indicate timing (hold, wait, now) through gesture speed
- **Error recovery** — have a specific gesture for "wrong, go back"
